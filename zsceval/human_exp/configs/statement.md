# EEG Human-AI Coordination Study

## Purpose
This study examines how people understand and coordinate with an AI teammate during a cooperative cooking game while EEG is recorded.

## Core Task
During the game, the participant will repeatedly see brief AI behavior and answer questions such as:

1. What subtask is the AI trying to do next?
2. What route do you expect the AI to take next?
3. After answering, how does the AI actually continue behaving?

The game interface uses three modes:

- **Observe**: watch an AI trajectory and answer two frozen expectation probes
- **Collaborate**: play with the AI and answer two frozen expectation probes during play
- **Replay + Annotation**: review recent scenes, pause twice, and capture what you expected before watching the continuation

At each probe, the participant chooses the expected next subtask and sketches the expected route. Probe answers are not explicitly marked correct or incorrect on screen. Instead, the task resumes so the participant can immediately see the AI's continuing behavior.

## Session Flow
The full session is expected to take roughly **90-120 minutes**.

1. Arrival / consent / screening: about 10 min
2. Onboarding briefing: about 5-7 min
3. EEG setup / baseline: about 10-20 min
4. Tutorial: about 10-15 min
5. Practice block: about 5-10 min
6. Main block 1 - Observe: about 10-15 min
7. Short survey + break: about 3-5 min
8. Main block 2 - Collaborate: about 10-15 min
9. Short survey + break: about 3-5 min
10. Main block 3 - Replay + annotation: about 10-15 min
11. Final post hoc survey: about 10 min
12. Debrief / interview: about 5 min

Each main interactive block contains **3 trials**. The hands-on tutorial stage includes a single short playthrough.

## Risks and Discomforts
The study involves normal computer use and EEG recording. EEG setup may involve mild inconvenience from wearing the cap and remaining relatively still.
